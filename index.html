Francesco Orsi

Data Science Manager·F. Hoffmann-La Roche

I began my career as a theoretical physicist, specializing in computational neuroscience, while working on projects of various scale across finance, healthcare, and regulatory compliance. During this time, I actively contributed to the research community through publications and open-source tools.

After several years of research, I pivoted to a field I am deeply passionate about — artificial intelligence governance and regulatory compliance. I transitioned from academia to industry, starting with data science roles at Swiss Federal Railways and later at Roche, pursuing leadership opportunities that complement technical depth with strategic business understanding.

Today, I lead a team of data scientists developing AI audit frameworks for regulated pharmaceutical environments, building comprehensive systems that use advanced machine learning, topological data analysis, and governance methodologies to move beyond deterministic checklists toward probabilistic, resilience-based assurance.

My long-term vision is to bridge healthcare and technology — connecting life sciences, government, and big tech to drive responsible innovation at scale in Europe and beyond.

I write regularly on Substack about applying advanced mathematical concepts — from differential geometry to Bayesian governance — to real-world audit challenges. I hold a PhD in Physics from CEA-Saclay / Université Paris VII-Diderot and over six years of research experience in computational neuroscience and machine learning.
